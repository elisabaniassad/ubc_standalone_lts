GRADEBOOK v2 — standalone. Everything stays on this machine; browser storage self-clears 2 hours after your last change (Download data to keep work longer); nothing leaves it except your exports.

Import a CSV (Student #, First, Last, Section, …one column per assessment…) or add columns and students by hand.
Formulas: SCALE(10), if(this>89,100), clamp(this,0,100), arithmetic on this or other columns' short codes.
Type *** as a column's short code or formula to make it THE course Raw column: it auto-names the column (e.g. Tentative Grade → TG) and writes that name into the Raw formula for you.
Grade formula (optional — nothing is applied unless you type it): ROUND()+BOOST (boost lifts a grade sitting within N of a UBC boundary), ROUNDUP/ROUNDDOWN, SCALE(n), +BOOST(2). The Grade column always reports whole numbers.
Parent folds: DROP(LOWEST[,n])/DROP(HIGHEST[,n]) · KEEP(HW5) protects members from the drop · SUM/MAX/MIN. Grade formula extras: BUMP(89->90,79->80) boosts those exact values only; or(TB,0) takes the first value that exists (so blank TB falls back to 0; orzero(TB) is shorthand) — e.g. raw formula +or(TB,0).
Click any grade in the marks table to edit it: change the mark, Exempt it (weight renormalizes), Replace the grade, or Move weight to another component — a reason is required for overrides, and every change appears in the OVERRIDES ledger beside the scheme.
A Final Exam column scoring exactly 0 flags DNW.
